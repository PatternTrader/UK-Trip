Day 1 · April 2
Arrive & Orient
  • ✈️ Fly in, check into family hotel (South Bank area)
  • 🌉 Evening stroll along the South Bank
  • 🏰 Views of Tower Bridge lit up at night
  • 🍽️ Dinner near Borough Market — relax after travel
Tip: Book a hotel with a kitchen or kitchenette — great for feeding 3 kids without restaurant stress every night.
Day 2 · April 3
Classic London Icons
  • 🗝️ Tower of London — Crown Jewels & Beefeaters
  • 🚢 Thames River Cruise to Westminster
  • 👑 Buckingham Palace — Changing of the Guard (11am)
  • 🌳 Hyde Park afternoon, Serpentine lake, Diana Playground
Tip: Changing of the Guard happens alternate days in spring — check the schedule in advance.
Day 3 · April 4
Museums & Westminster
  • 🏛️ British Museum — Rosetta Stone & mummies (FREE)
  • ⛪ Westminster Abbey — Poets' Corner & royal tombs
  • 🕰️ Big Ben & Parliament Square (great photo stop)
  • 🎨 South Bank: Tate Modern + Millennium Bridge walk
Tip: British Museum is massive — focus on Egyptian & Greek galleries with kids. Allow 2–3 hrs minimum.

Stonehenge
April 5 — depart 8am, arrive ~10am
5,000 years old and still one of the world's greatest mysteries. Awe-inspiring for every age.
Day 4 · April 5
Stonehenge & White Cliffs of Dover
  • 🗿 Stonehenge (Wiltshire) — depart 8am, arrive ~10am, allow 2 hrs
  • 🚗 Drive east to White Cliffs of Dover (~2 hrs from Stonehenge)
  • 🌊 Walk the cliff path — breathtaking Channel views
  • 🏠 Return to London via M20/M25 — back by 7pm
Tip: Rent a car for this day — public transport to both sites on the same day is very difficult. Pre-book Stonehenge tickets!
Day 5 · April 6
Stratford-upon-Avon
  • 🎭 Shakespeare's Birthplace — guided family tour
  • 🌸 Anne Hathaway's Cottage — beautiful thatched garden
  • 🦢 River Avon walk & swan feeding — kids love this
  • 🚗 ~2 hrs from London — depart 8am, return by 7pm
Tip: Stratford is northwest of London — perfect to visit the day before heading north to Scotland.

April 7 – 9

Edinburgh

3 days · Royal Mile base · Castle · Stirling day trip
Scotland's #1 Attraction
Edinburgh Castle
April 8 — morning
Perched on volcanic rock above the city, housing Scotland's Crown Jewels and the One O'Clock Gun.
Old Town
The Royal Mile
April 8 — afternoon
Day Trip
Stirling Castle
April 9
Day 6 · April 7
LNER Train to Edinburgh
  • 🚄 LNER from London Kings Cross → Edinburgh Waverley (4.5 hrs)
  • 🏔️ Stunning scenery through Yorkshire & Northumberland
  • 🏨 Arrive midday — check into hotel near Royal Mile
  • 🍺 Afternoon: Grassmarket, Victoria Street, local pub dinner
Tip: Book LNER tickets 8–12 weeks ahead for best family prices. Reserve 4 seats around a table — perfect for kids.
Day 7 · April 8
Edinburgh Castle & Old Town
  • 🏯 Edinburgh Castle — Crown Jewels, One O'Clock Gun, great views
  • ⚔️ Royal Mile walk from Castle down to Holyrood Palace
  • 👑 Palace of Holyroodhouse — official Scottish royal residence
  • ⛰️ Arthur's Seat — optional easy hike, panoramic city views
Tip: Edinburgh Castle can take 2–3 hrs with kids. Book ahead — it sells out in spring.
Day 8 · April 9
Stirling Castle Day Trip
  • 🏰 Stirling Castle — ~45 min from Edinburgh, spectacular hilltop fortress
  • 🗡️ Wallace Monument & Battle of Stirling Bridge site
  • 🎮 Bannockburn Heritage Centre — interactive, great for kids!
  • 🌃 Return to Edinburgh — evening on Grassmarket or Princes Street
Tip: Stirling is perfectly between Edinburgh and the Highlands — visit now to head straight north the next day.

One of Scotland's most dramatic landscapes — a sweeping glacial valley flanked by towering mountains.
Isle of Skye Route
Eilean Donan Castle
April 11 — en route to Skye
Scotland's most photographed castle, on a tiny island where three lochs meet.
Isle of Skye
Old Man of Storr
April 11 — afternoon
Otherworldly rock pinnacles rising above the mist — one of Scotland's most iconic viewpoints.
Isle of Skye
Fairy Pools
April 11 — morning
Crystal-clear blue pools fed by waterfalls cascading down from the Cuillin mountains. Magical for kids.
Highland Wildlife
Highland Cattle 🐄
Spotted all throughout the Highlands!
The shaggy, long-horned coos of Scotland. You'll spot them grazing along roadsides — kids absolutely love them.
Day 9 · April 10
Loch Ness & The Highlands
  • 🚗 Rent car in Edinburgh — drive north through Pitlochry
  • 🦕 Loch Ness — Urquhart Castle ruins on the loch shore
  • 🎟️ Loch Ness Centre & Exhibition — kids will love the monster lore
  • ⛰️ Drive through Glencoe valley — Scotland's most dramatic landscape
Tip: Stay overnight in Inverness or Fort William — best base for Highlands & Skye. Book accommodation well ahead for April.
Day 10 · April 11
Isle of Skye
  • 🏰 Drive to Skye via Eilean Donan Castle (iconic photo stop!)
  • 🧚 Fairy Pools — magical blue rock pools, easy family walk
  • 🗿 Old Man of Storr — otherworldly rock pinnacles, short hike
  • 🌅 Neist Point Lighthouse — stunning cliffside sunset views
Tip: Skye is ~2.5 hrs from Inverness. The island is bigger than it looks — pick 2–3 spots rather than trying to see everything.
Day 11 · April 12
Fly Home
  • 🚗 Drive back from Skye/Inverness to Edinburgh (~3 hrs)
  • ✈️ Fly from Edinburgh Airport — many direct routes
  • ⏰ Arrive airport 2.5 hrs early with a family of 5
  • 💭 Reflect on an incredible adventure!
Alternative: Fly from London instead — take the LNER back from Edinburgh on Apr 12 morning and fly from Heathrow for more flight options.

Practical Guide

Family Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before you go
🎟️
Book These in Advance
Tower of London — sells out in spring
Stonehenge — MUST pre-book timed entry
Edinburgh Castle — book 2–3 weeks ahead
LNER Train — cheapest 8–12 weeks ahead
🚄
LNER Train to Edinburgh
London Kings Cross → Edinburgh Waverley in 4.5 hrs.
Book as early as possible for the best family prices. Reserve 4 seats around a table — perfect for keeping kids entertained on the journey.
🚗
Rent a Car Twice
Day trip (Apr 5): London → Stonehenge → White Cliffs → return
Highlands (Apr 10–11): Rent in Edinburgh for Loch Ness, Glencoe & Isle of Skye
In London use the Oyster card on Tube & buses.
🌦️
April Weather
Expect 8–13°C (46–55°F) — pack layers for everyone.
Rain is likely, especially in Scotland — waterproof jackets are essential.
On the bright side: daylight until ~8pm and fewer crowds than summer!
💷
Currency
The UK uses British Pounds (£ GBP).
Contactless card payment is accepted almost everywhere.
Many London museums are completely free (British Museum, Natural History Museum, Tate Modern).
🏨
Where to Stay
London: South Bank or Marylebone — family rooms or serviced apartments
Edinburgh: Royal Mile or New Town area
Highlands: Inverness — great base for Loch Ness & Skye
Book early! April is busy in Edinburgh.
